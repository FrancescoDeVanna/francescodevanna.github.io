I supervise **M.Sc. theses and Ph.D. projects** (I do not currently offer B.Sc. theses) and welcome motivated students interested in computational fluid dynamics, propulsion and high-performance computing. Below are the main areas where I currently offer projects — specific topics are tailored together, based on your background and interests.

## Thesis areas (M.Sc.)

- **High-fidelity simulation of gas-turbine flows** — wall-modeled/wall-resolved LES of transonic vanes, shock/boundary-layer interaction and **film cooling**, using the [URANOS](/portfolio/uranos/) solver on EuroHPC systems.
- **GPU computing and HPC development** — porting and optimizing CFD kernels (Fortran/OpenACC) for modern accelerators, performance analysis on pre-exascale machines.
- **CFD-driven design and optimization** — turbomachinery and propulsion components: propellers, waterjets, intakes and diffusers, using surrogate-assisted and multi-level optimization frameworks.
- **Machine learning for fluid dynamics** — data-driven surrogates for aerothermal design and reduced-order modeling of turbulent flows.

**What helps**: curiosity first; some familiarity with Python and/or Fortran and basic CFD is a plus for the computational topics, but can be built along the way.

**How to get in touch**: write to [francesco.devanna@unipd.it](mailto:francesco.devanna@unipd.it) with a short note on your interests, your transcript and — if you have one — a CV. We will then meet to shape a topic together.

## Ph.D. opportunities

My research lines (compressible turbulence, film cooling, GPU-based solvers, ML for fluids) are open to doctoral students through the doctoral programmes of the University of Padova. Admissions go exclusively through the official calls:

- [Ph.D. programmes — University of Padova](https://www.unipd.it/en/phd-programmes)
- [CISAS "G. Colombo" — Center of Studies and Activities for Space](https://cisas.unipd.it/)

If you are considering applying and my research fits your interests, feel free to reach out beforehand to discuss potential projects.
