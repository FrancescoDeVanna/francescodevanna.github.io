# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Academic peers, journal editors, conference organizers, prospective PhD students and postdocs, industry R&D contacts, and university committees. They arrive from Google Scholar, a paper byline, or a search of the name — typically on desktop, often skimming for 30–60 seconds to answer: "who is this researcher, what do they work on, how do I cite/contact them?"

## Product Purpose

Personal academic site of Francesco De Vanna, Tenure-Track Assistant Professor in Aerospace Propulsion (University of Padova & CISAS), lead developer of the URANOS GPU solver. The site is the canonical, self-owned home for bio, publications (ORCID-synced), and CV. Success = a visitor finds an authoritative, current picture of the research in under a minute, and the site never reads as commercial or as speaking for the university.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, understated, precise. The voice of a computational scientist: facts over adjectives, typography over decoration. Confidence through completeness (63 publications, real DOIs), not through marketing language.

## Anti-references

- Startup/consultant landing pages: no hero taglines, no CTAs, no service offerings, no testimonials.
- The default unstyled academicpages look (competent but generic).
- Any layout or wording that leverages the University of Padova name promotionally — the affiliation is stated as biographical fact only (Codice Etico Unipd, art. 9: no institutional logo, no university reputation associated with external activities, personal views not attributable to the university).

## Design Principles

1. **Compliance is a design constraint** — factual affiliation, personal-views footer disclaimer, no Unipd logo/colors that could imply institutional identity.
2. **The publication list is the product** — everything else supports fast scanning of the research record.
3. **Refined, not redesigned** — keep the academicpages skeleton; elevate typography, spacing, and hierarchy so it reads as deliberate.
4. **No empty rooms** — sections without real content stay out of the navigation.
5. **Self-contained and fast** — static Jekyll, no trackers beyond defaults, works perfectly on GitHub Pages.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, visible focus states, semantic headings, alt text on the profile image. Reduced-motion respected (site is essentially static). English-language content; hyphenation/line-length tuned for long technical titles.
